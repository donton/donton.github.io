Introduction

Campus Events + Entertainment (E+E) is the student programming board of The University of Texas at Austin. E+E is the largest student planning organization on campus with over 250 members and 10 committees focused on planning and executing different types of events.

With over 100 events a year, E+E has a large scope across campus and reaches tens of thousands of students yearly. However, with an aging website last updated nearly a decade ago in 2009, students are looking for a modern and interactive way of getting information about events and the organization.

Current Website

The current website was designed and developed to display information without user experience in mind. Users are expected to comb through the site to find event and organization information, but they are often left with distaste after losing patience trying to find what they want.

Discovery & Research

My discovery and research stage consisted of determining goals for the site, taking a look at best practices across other universities and most importantly, talking to the users to understand their situtations, wishes, as well as likes and dislikes.

Goals

  • Have organization information available
  • Have event information available
  • Allow advisors/chairs to easily update content
  • Make it more aesthetically pleasing

User Interviews

Interviews that I conducted were crucial in understanding what was important to users across the spectrum. Users include students, members, committee chairs and advisors.

Primary Uses

View the calendar and event details
Find committee meeting times and locations

Wishes

Better committee information
Modern format with more graphics

Likes

Social media integration

Dislikes

Text Heavy
Difficult to find information

Everyone stated that they usually go to Facebook to find event information instead of the website. This should be the other way around.

Competition

Because university student programming is something most schools have, it was easy to find helpful examples of other websites.

User Analysis

After conducting research and getting to know what users are looking for, I created User Personas to depict each type of user and analyzed the steps they would take on the site.

User Personas

Jason Aldeer

First Year Student at the University of Texas

“I’m looking to spend my free time doing something here on campus.”

Goals

  • Find something to do during his free time
  • Make new friends
  • Gain new leadership experiences

Frustrations

  • Organizations with an inconsistent roster of members
  • Poorly organized student groups

Jason is a first year student at the University of Texas. Overwhelmed by the new pressures of college, he would like to find something to do with his time when he’s not doing schoolwork. He knows of the free events for students on campus and would like to find more information on that. Looking to make friends, Jason has found some interesting student organizations, but he is unwilling to commit because they are poorly organized and have different people showing up to meetings every week. He wants to join something that will be valuable to his time.

"   I would like to be able to easily find information about events on campus and how they happen because I’d like to be a part of them myself "

April Santiago

Chair of Showtime, Committee within Campus Events + Entertainment

“I want a more effective way to showcase my committee and our events.”

Goals

  • Showcase committee with information and media
  • Advertise upcoming events
  • Get information about other committees’ events
  • Recruit members to join committee

Frustrations

  • Outdated information on the website
  • Lack of information

April is the Chair (President) of Showtime, a committee within Campus Events+Entertainment that shows movies on campus every week. As Chair, it is her responsibility to showcase her committee and its events while recruiting members to join throughout the semester. April is not happy with the current status of the E+E website as it has outdated information. She really wishes she could just change it herself to reflect how great the committee she is in charge of is, but there are too many hoops to jump through! She often uses the website to figure out upcoming events for other committees to tell her members about, but it can be a better experience overall.

"   I would like to have information about my organization its events easily accessible because I want more people to be a part of them "

Caroline Alazar

Campus Events + Entertainment Advisor

“I want to empower my student leaders and members and give them a tool to help them make an impact.”

Goals

  • Have updated information about organization and events on website for students
  • Update information on website easily
  • Have forms and other resources available for members

Frustrations

  • Navigating through difficult software just to update content
  • Lack of student engagement

As an advisor for Campus Events + Entertainment, it’s Caroline’s job to be a resource for students and members of the organization. She would like the organization to be an enriching place for students to thrive as leaders and have fun. Along with that, it is part of her mission to help them plan and execute exciting events on campus for all students. She would like a website that is resourceful for everyone who accesses it - students, members and faculty. Caroline thinks the current design is lacking and that information should be organized more graphically to draw in more people.

"   I would like for our information and resources to be readily available to anyone who needs it because that would make the event-planning process better "

Homepage & About

Since this is the landing page for most users, I wanted to quickly draw users into the featured information in the hero section by making this section large filling the screen.

The UP NEXT section showcases the next upcoming event, something that many users may find helpful.

Social media is also integrated into the homepage and the welcome section features a call-to-action button that leads the user to the About Us page.

The About Us page cleanly describes the organization, how to get involved, its leadership and how to get in contact.

Upcoming Events

I believe Upcoming Events is the most important aspect of the website. Users are more likely to be drawn in by the posters of the events rather than just a text headline.

Events are displayed graphically with their poster image and users have the ability to display them as tiles or as a list with more details.

The event details page contains more information and a Facebook Event page link, something users find valuable.

Committee Pages

The committee page graphically lists all of the committees with their details and short description.

Within a committee's page are featured images, more details, a more thorough description, the next event and past events.

This format is consistent across all committee pages, unlike the current version. This keeps the user more engaged and makes it easier to find information.

Final Thoughts

Working on the Campus Events + Entertainment site was an enjoyable experience for me. With a site catered towards college students and young adults, I was able to take advantage of digital elements that draw them in and keep them engaged. This site is definitely an improvement from the last, and it doesn't overload the user with too much information. Everything is right where you expect to find it under the three main pages – About, Upcoming Events and Committees. Part of the purpose of the website is to persuade students to attend the organization's events. By taking advantage of this site, students will be more encouraged to actually go to the events with updated information easily available.

I have received good feedback from the prototype with small suggestions for changes here and there. I was able to hand off my design and work to the Director for Student Programs and a new and updated site should be developed within the coming months. I have learned a lot doing this project on my own about the entire UX Design process and how to cater to different types of users simultaneously. In the future, I will develop my user testing skills to make the process more formal and have a better system to document it. All in all, it was a wonderful experience for me.
