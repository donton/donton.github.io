Kayak Usability Test Plan

Goal

Create a usability test plan for Kayak.com

Process

Understand the existing site, research user needs, determine test subjects, define success metrics

My Role

UX Research

Introduction

KAYAK is a travel search engine that looks across the web to find deals on flights, hotels and rental cars. Unlike most competitors, it searches across multiple services to try and find the lowest price – something that draws a lot of users in.

Users of the site range from young to old and represent a diverse group of people. Because Kayak's purpose is to sell, it is especially important to cater to users across the spectrum. I created a test plan to evaluate the user experience on the site by establishing a baseline of user performance, establishing and validating user performance measures, and identifying potential design concerns to be addressed in order to improve the efficiency, productivity and end-user satisfaction.

View Test Plan

Final Thoughts

KAYAK, being the popular service it is, has been tested and designed for users in mind. My task was to create an effective test plan that considered a diverse range of people looking to perform different actions. The most challenging part of this project was determining metrics of success. I had to create definitions of success that assessed things other than completion. Creating a usability test plan for such a high profile site allowed me to follow and understand the steps a modern site would take to improve usability.
