My Role

UI Design

Introduction

Wingspan is a travel app that saves people time at the airport. After a user inputs their flight number, the app determines the most ideal time to leave for the airport based on their current location. The app also provides all necessary flight information with live updates notifying the user if there has been a change. Wingspan is the perfect companion for a busy traveler.

Wingspan is a concept that I developed out of frastration with air travel. I often find myself at the airport with either too much time just sitting around waiting or with too little time scrambling to make my flight. I wanted to find that sweet in-between; that's what Wingspan provides. The app suggests the perfect time to leave while displaying all relevant flight information in one place. This was my journey designing the app.

Discovery & Research

My discovery and research stage primarily consisted of defining the problem and then determining functionality to solve the problem.

The Problem

  • People aren't at the airport for the right amount of time – they get there too early with a lot of leftover time or they get there too late and are pressed on time
  • They don't know where to go exactly for their flight – airport, terminal, gate, etc.
  • They're not updated on their flight and any changes – delays, gate changes, etc.

The Solution

  • A travel app that tells the user when to leave for the airport to catch a flight or to pick someone up after their flight has landed
  • Just needs the flight number and current location
  • Link to Google Maps with driving directions
  • Flight information accessible through the app
  • Alerts on when to leave, traffic updates, flight updates, etc.

When and where do you go to catch your flight? What's the perfect time to leave?

Sketches

With user-functionality in mind, I sketched up the app's main screens – a welcome screen, the flight input screen and the flight information screen with information on when to leave.

Wireframes

Based on the sketches, I created these wireframes to further illustrate the UI. The flight input screen now shows suggestions as the user types in the flight number. I also broke up the flight information screen into two screens: one with information about when to leave and one with flight information.

The When to Leave screen with alert cards that pop up.

Final Design

The wireframes helped to define my final design. These four screens encompass the range of the app. I also included my brand design for Wingspan.

Welcome Screen

This is the screen the user sees when they open the app for the first time. It introduces them to the purpose of Wingspan in a simple way.

There is potential to add a login option for the user in the future just in case they wanted to save their flights and see other flights.

Flight Input Screen

This screen prompts the user to enter in their departing or arriving flight number - the cases for catching a flight and picking someone up.

As the user types, flight suggestions pop up, and the user selects their flight once they see it. By forcing the user to select only a flight on the screen, it prevents the app from receiving an incorrect flight number.

When to Leave Screen

The main screen of the app simply displays when the user should leave along with a map of the route.

The two buttons at the bottom allow the user to change their starting location as well as open the route in their maps application to get directions.

If any traffic or flight updates come up, the alerts will pop in next to the countdown prompting the user to take notice.

If users change their mind, they can change the flight by clicking on the flight number at the top.

Flight Information

A simple swipe from the right reveals the flight information screen. Here, users can find all of their necessary flight information such as departure and arrive times and cities, terminal, gate and baggage claim numbers as well as much more.

Users using the app to pick someone up can track the progress of the incoming flight with the route map and easily find the terminal map of the airport.

Brand Design

Before designing the final UI, I designed Wingspan's logo and brand scheme.

Final Thoughts

Wingspan started out as a simple frustration, I didn't want to waste time at the airport. I think this is an issue people face everyday, so I wanted to solve it with this app concept. My challenge was to design a simple and intuitive user interface that didn't make the already hectic travel experience more complicated. I had to design an experience that would aid a traveler and make their journey simpler.

The idea behind the app was simple, to display when you should leave for the airport. I made sure to make this the focal point and add supplementary information around it. My most difficult task was laying out all the different flight information in a clean and helpful way. Most flight status trackers are bogged down with a bunch of text that make it difficult to find what you need and go. My design highlights key information in a light way. With the final design ready to go, the app is one step closer to becoming a reality.
